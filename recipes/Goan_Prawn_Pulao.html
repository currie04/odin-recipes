Goan Prawn Pulao

Description

Goan prawn pulao is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish originating from the coastal region of Goa, India. It features plump, juicy prawns cooked with basmati rice, infused with the rich flavors of coconut milk and a medley of spices such as turmeric, coriander, cumin, and black pepper.

The dish is typically garnished with fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice, enhancing its fresh and vibrant taste. The combination of aromatic spices, tender prawns, and creamy coconut milk creates a harmonious and satisfying meal that reflects Goa's culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  • Prawns
  • Basmati Rice
  • Coconut Milk
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Green Chillies
  • Garlic
  • Ginger

Preparation

  1. Marination: Marinate the prawns with turmeric, chili powder, and salt to enhance their flavor.
  2. Cooking the Rice: Par-cook or fully cook the basmati rice separately to ensure it remains fluffy and does not become mushy.
  3. Sautéing: Sauté onions, green chilies, and tomatoes in ghee or oil until they are soft and aromatic. Add garlic and ginger paste for additional flavor.
  4. Spices: Add whole spices like cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom to the sautéed mixture to release their aromas.
  5. Cooking the Prawns: Add the marinated prawns to the sautéed mixture and cook until they are just done.
  6. Combining: Mix in the par-cooked or fully cooked rice with the prawn mixture. Pour in coconut milk and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper.
  7. Simmering: Cover and simmer the mixture on low heat until the rice is fully cooked and has absorbed the flavors.
  8. Garnishing: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice before serving.
